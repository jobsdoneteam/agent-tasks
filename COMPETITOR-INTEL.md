## TOP COMPLAINTS BY COMPETITOR

### Broadly
- **Bait and switch billing**: Customers report unauthorized charges on new credit card numbers after canceling — one user had to replace 2 cards after Broadly somehow charged a brand new account number
- **Pushy sales**: Reps pressure sign-ups and payment before prospects can evaluate the product
- **Broken promises**: Sales reps overpromise features; customers charged $200/mo same day after being promised a 30-day no-obligation trial
- **Almost no public social proof**: Only 2 Trustpilot reviews, both 1-star. Tiny brand awareness outside their marketing
- **Feature gating**: AI SMS, AI voice receptionist, and review requesting are all locked behind $699-$999 plans

### Podium
- **Contract trap**: 12-month auto-renewing contracts; BBB complaint from customer charged $570/mo for service they tried to cancel over a year prior
- **Sales harassment**: BBB documented 3+ years of unwanted calls/texts to a business that simply declined to sign up
- **No AI review responses**: Uses templates only — Trustpilot and G2 reviewers call this out explicitly vs. competitors
- **Feature bloat**: Small businesses pay $400-600/mo but use roughly 20% of features
- **Weak AI webchat**: Trustpilot reviewers (Feb 2026) note AI often does not directly answer customer questions
- **61 BBB complaints** in 3 years; 18 in the last 12 months alone (trending up)

### Birdeye
- **Cancellation nightmare**: Multiple BBB complaints of charges continuing after cancellation — customers ignored for 3-9 months across tickets, calls, and emails
- **90-day cancellation notice buried in contract**: Miss it and you are auto-renewed for another full 12 months
- **Feature removal mid-contract**: Capabilities like referrals included at signup, then moved behind a higher paywall with no warning
- **Hidden pricing**: Must submit company details before seeing a quote; actual costs run 40-60% higher than initial quote due to required add-ons
- **Social is a top-tier upsell**: $449/mo Dominate plan required just to access social media posting
- **Support requires BBB complaint**: Multiple G2, Trustpilot, and ComplaintsBoard reviewers confirm they had to file a formal BBB complaint to get a callback
- **110 BBB complaints** in 3 years; 36 in the last 12 months (accelerating)

### Thryv
- **Fully opaque pricing**: Zero public numbers — everything requires a sales call
- **Yellow Pages legacy**: Owned and built by the former Yellow Pages company; brand trust issues with tech-aware small business owners
- **All-in-one sprawl**: Steep learning curve consistently reported; too broad to be excellent at anything specific
- **Enterprise friction on small business budgets**: Positions as SMB software but operates with enterprise-level contracts and onboarding complexity

### Wix
- **Auto-renewal price shock**: Renewals jump 30-195% with minimal notice; one verified user renewed from $210 to $620, another was charged $900 after manually disabling auto-renewal
- **14-day refund policy strictly enforced**: No exceptions even when Wix support gives wrong instructions or makes errors — customers forfeit remaining months if they cancel mid-year
- **Payment holds**: E-commerce merchants report funds held 30-60+ days with no resolution path; some lost thousands in business while waiting
- **Template lock-in**: Switching templates requires a complete site rebuild from scratch
- **Slow load times hurt SEO**: Wix averages 3.4 second load time vs 1.2 seconds for WordPress — direct impact on Google rankings
- **Support black hole**: Reddit community warns you will never talk to an actual Wix employee; only 29% of customers successfully reach human support
- **Rating gap**: G2/Capterra 4.2-4.4 stars (professional buyers) vs Sitejabber 1.3 stars (real users with real problems)
